The Ultimate Guide to Digital Signatures in PDFs

Digital signatures have completely transformed how we handle documents. No more printing, signing with a pen, scanning, and emailing back. But what exactly is a digital signature, and how does it differ from simply typing your name or drawing it on a screen?

What is a Digital Signature?

A digital signature is a mathematical technique used to validate the authenticity and integrity of a message, software, or digital document. In PDFs, it ensures that the document wasn't tampered with after it was signed.

Are They Legally Binding?

Yes, in most countries (including the US with the ESIGN Act, and the EU with eIDAS), digital signatures carry the same legal weight as traditional wet-ink signatures.

How to Add a Digital Signature

Using advanced PDF tools, you can easily insert a cryptographic signature. Simply select the document, choose your digital certificate, and place the signature block on the page.
