The Importance of PDF/A for Long-Term Document Archiving

If you're saving a document that needs to be perfectly readable 10, 20, or even 50 years from now, a standard PDF might not be enough. Technology changes rapidly, and you need a format designed specifically for longevity. Enter PDF/A.

What is PDF/A?

PDF/A (Portable Document Format for Archiving) is an ISO-standardized version of the PDF format. It prohibits features that are ill-suited for long-term archiving, such as font linking (as opposed to font embedding) and encryption.

Key Restrictions of PDF/A

  • 100% Embedded Fonts: The document must contain all the font information needed to render the text. It cannot rely on the operating system having the font installed.
  • No Audio or Video: Multimedia elements are banned, as the software to play them may not exist in the future.
  • No Encryption: Passwords are not allowed, because if the password is lost over decades, the archival data is destroyed.
  • Color Management: Color spaces must be strictly defined to ensure the document looks the same on future displays.

When to Use It

PDF/A should be used for legal records, historical archives, final academic theses, and long-term business contracts. Converting your standard PDFs to PDF/A guarantees their survival for generations.
