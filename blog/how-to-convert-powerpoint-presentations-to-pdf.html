How to Convert PowerPoint Presentations to PDF

You've spent hours perfecting the fonts, layout, and graphics of your PowerPoint presentation. But when you email it to your client, they open it on a Mac, and the fonts are swapped out, the text boxes overlap, and the formatting is ruined. The solution? Convert your presentation to a PDF.

Why PDF is Better for Slide Decks

While PowerPoint is the ultimate tool for creating slides and presenting them live, it's terrible for distributing them. PDFs lock your design in place. Every font, image, and shape will appear exactly as you designed it, regardless of whether the recipient is using a PC, a Mac, a tablet, or a smartphone.

What You Lose in Conversion

It's important to note that PDFs are static. When you convert a.pptx to a.pdf, you will lose:

  • Slide transition animations (like fades or wipes).
  • On-slide object animations (like text flying in).
  • Embedded video playback.

If your presentation relies heavily on these features, a PDF might not be the best choice. But if your goal is to provide a clean, readable handout or reference deck, converting to PDF is the professional standard.
