    Frequently Asked Questions

    Q. What is a WebP file?

    WebP is a modern image format developed by Google that provides superior compression for images on the web. It supports both lossy and lossless compression, transparency, and animation. Many websites use WebP to load pages faster, but not all software and devices can open WebP files natively.

    Q. How to convert WebP to JPG on Windows?

    Simply open this tool in any browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge), drag and drop your WebP files, and click "Convert." No software installation needed. Works on Windows 10, 11, Mac, Linux, and all other operating systems.

    Q. Can I convert multiple WebP files at once?

    Yes! You can batch convert up to 30 WebP files simultaneously. Select multiple files or drag and drop a batch. Download all converted JPGs as a single ZIP file.

    Q. Is it safe to convert my images here?

    Absolutely! Your images never leave your device. All conversion happens locally inside your browser using HTML5 Canvas technology. No data is sent to any server. Your privacy is fully protected.

    Q. Why can't I open WebP images on my computer?

    Older versions of Windows Photo Viewer, macOS Preview, and many desktop applications don't support WebP natively. While newer OS versions have added support, converting to JPG guarantees the image will open on any device or software.

    Q. Does it work on mobile phones?

    Yes! OnlinePDFPro works perfectly on all devices — Android phones, iPhones, tablets, laptops and desktops. No app download needed.
